Why Traditional Ecommerce Creates Friction

Digital commerce has fundamentally not changed in twenty years. Buyers still arrive at a website, type keywords into a search bar, scroll through result pages, apply filters, click into product detail pages, open multiple browser tabs to compare, and eventually either check out or leave. The entire burden of the buying decision falls on the buyer.

This model creates friction at every stage:

Traditional Ecommerce

  • 1Search with keywords
  • 2Navigate product pages
  • 3Compare options manually
  • 4Fill forms and configure
  • 5Contact sales or abandon

Touchless Commerce

  • 1Express need in plain language
  • 2AI understands intent
  • 3AI recommends with reasons
  • 4AI configures and acts
  • 5Purchase complete

The consequences of friction are measurable. Average ecommerce cart abandonment rates exceed 70%. Buyers spend hours researching and still leave without deciding. Support teams are overwhelmed with questions that product pages should have answered. Brands invest in better search and better pages, but the core problem that buyers must do all the work remains unchanged.

Touchless commerce addresses this differently. Rather than improving the existing browsing model, it replaces the browsing requirement entirely the AI handles navigation, comparison, and purchase completion on behalf of the buyer.

Touchless Commerce Definition

Touchless commerce is a digital commerce model where AI systems guide buyers through the purchasing journey without requiring manual browsing, filtering, or complex navigation. Buyers interact with an intelligent system that understands their needs, recommends products, configures options, and completes transactions. The goal of touchless commerce is to remove friction from the buying process and reduce the effort required to reach a purchase decision.

The name "touchless" refers not to removing physical touch it refers to removing the friction points that buyers currently have to "touch" manually: search bars, filter menus, comparison tables, checkout forms, configuration pages, and contact forms. In touchless commerce, the AI handles all of these steps.

The buyer's experience collapses from a multi-step, multi-page process into a single continuous conversation. The effort required to go from "I need something" to "I have bought it" approaches zero.

How Touchless Commerce Works

Touchless commerce works through a five-step AI-guided process. At every step, the AI handles what would traditionally require manual buyer effort.

1

Buyer Expresses Intent

The buyer states what they need in natural, unstructured language no keywords, no filters, no forms. The AI accepts any phrasing.

"I need a refrigerator for a small apartment, quiet, under $900"
2

AI Understands Intent

The AI extracts the buyer's underlying requirements: budget constraints, size requirements, lifestyle preferences, and unstated priorities. It builds a precise buyer profile from the conversation no form required.

3

Product Discovery

The AI searches the product catalog against the buyer's requirements not keyword matching, but genuine compatibility matching. It narrows thousands of SKUs to the two or three that truly fit.

4

Recommendations with Trade-offs

The AI explains why each shortlisted option fits the buyer's specific situation, what the trade-offs are between options, and which one it recommends and why. The buyer can make a confident decision without opening a single product page.

5

Action and Completion

The AI completes the next step autonomously booking a demo, scheduling a delivery, placing an order, or configuring the final product. The buying journey is complete within the conversation.

"Order placed. Delivery scheduled for Thursday. Confirmation sent to your email."

What the Buying Experience Looks Like: Before and After

The difference between traditional ecommerce and touchless commerce is not a feature difference — it is an entirely different sequence of what the buyer has to do to reach a purchase decision.

Buying a car online: traditional ecommerce

  1. Land on homepage. Navigate to "Cars" section.
  2. Apply filters: body type, fuel type, price range, seating capacity.
  3. Browse 12 results. Open 4 in separate tabs.
  4. Read 4 spec sheets. Struggle to compare range figures across different test standards.
  5. Search Google: "BYD Atto 3 vs MG ZS EV real-world range UAE."
  6. Visit 2 competitor websites to cross-reference.
  7. Return to original site. Try to calculate EMI using a third-party loan calculator.
  8. Fill out a "Request a callback" form.
  9. Wait 24 hours for a salesperson to call.
  10. Abandon if no callback by end of day.

Result: Buyer spent 40+ minutes. Most leave without completing step 8. Those who do still face a 24-hour gap before the next action.

Buying a car online: touchless commerce

  1. Land on product page. AI agent activates based on scroll behavior.
  2. Buyer types (or speaks): "I need a family car, 5 seats, worried about range in the UAE summer."
  3. AI agent responds with two specific models, explains real-world range at 40°C, and shows a side-by-side comparison card mid-conversation.
  4. Buyer asks: "What's the monthly payment with AED 50,000 down?" AI calculates EMI inline.
  5. Buyer says: "Book me a test drive for Saturday." AI confirms booking without leaving the page.

Result: Buyer spent 8 minutes. Every question was answered in the session. Test drive booked before exit intent triggered.

The behavioral change

  • Average session time: from 4 minutes (static pages) to 7 minutes with AI agent active — buyers stay longer because they are getting answers, not hitting walls
  • Engagement rate: from ~4% on static product pages to 27% when touchless commerce is active
  • Test-drive conversions: 5× uplift when hesitation is resolved in-session vs. deferred to a callback
  • Tab-switching: eliminated — the buyer never leaves the brand's page to cross-reference competitors

The shift from traditional ecommerce to touchless commerce is not an incremental improvement — it is a fundamental change in who does the work during a purchase. Traditional ecommerce makes the buyer do the work. Touchless commerce transfers that work to AI.

Touchless Commerce vs Conversational Commerce

Touchless commerce is often compared to conversational commerce, but the two are distinct in scope and outcome.

Conversational commerce refers to the use of messaging interfaces chatbots, live chat, social messaging to assist buyers. The focus is on communication: answering questions, providing support, guiding buyers through what already exists. Conversational commerce improves the experience around traditional ecommerce without replacing it.

Touchless commerce goes further. It uses AI not just to communicate but to act to conduct the entire buying journey on behalf of the buyer. The buyer does not navigate to a product page; the AI navigates for them. The buyer does not compare options; the AI compares and recommends. The buyer does not fill a form; the AI completes it. The result is a commerce experience with no manual friction points at all.

Conversational Commerce Touchless Commerce
Chat-based assistance Full AI-guided buying journey
Support and basic queries Discovery, comparison, configuration, transaction
Reduces friction around existing ecommerce Eliminates friction entirely
Communication layer Complete buying experience

In short: conversational commerce is communication. Touchless commerce is the full buying experience without friction.

AI Sales Agents in Touchless Commerce

Without an AI sales agent, touchless commerce cannot function. The agent is the component that removes friction by replacing manual browsing with guided conversation, replacing manual comparison with AI-driven recommendations, and replacing form-filling with autonomous action.

An effective AI sales agent in a touchless commerce system must be:

  • Intent-aware understanding what the buyer means, not just what they typed
  • Product-knowledgeable trained on real buyer signals, not just brand descriptions
  • Action-capable able to navigate pages, configure products, and complete bookings
  • Always available operating 24/7 across all digital channels and languages

The quality of the touchless commerce experience is directly determined by the quality of the AI sales agent powering it.

Touchless Commerce and Agentic Commerce

Touchless commerce and agentic commerce are closely related but describe different dimensions of the same shift in digital buying.

Touchless Commerce

Describes the buyer experience a frictionless journey from intent to purchase, with no manual browsing, filtering, or forms required. It is the outcome that buyers perceive.

Agentic Commerce

Describes the AI architecture autonomous agents that act on behalf of buyers to complete purchase journeys. It is the mechanism that delivers the touchless experience.

The simplest framing: touchless commerce is what the buyer experiences. Agentic commerce is what powers it. A brand deploying an agentic commerce platform is, from the buyer's perspective, delivering a touchless commerce experience.

Both concepts represent the same macro shift in digital commerce: moving from buyer-driven browsing to AI-driven guidance. Touchless commerce names the destination; agentic commerce names the vehicle that gets there.

The Future of Touchless Commerce

Touchless commerce is in its early stages. The current generation of AI sales agents reduces friction significantly the next generation will eliminate it almost entirely. Several clear trends define where this is heading:

  • AI-native commerce interfaces. The product grid, the filter sidebar, the checkout page these will give way to AI-first interfaces where conversation is the primary purchasing surface. Especially on mobile and voice devices.
  • Voice-first buying. Buyers will complete high-value purchases entirely through voice describing needs, receiving recommendations, and confirming purchases without touching a screen.
  • Cross-channel continuity. A single buyer intent expressed on one channel web, app, email, WhatsApp, in-store will be picked up and continued seamlessly across all of them by the same AI agent with full context.
  • Proactive touchless journeys. AI agents will initiate buying conversations based on predicted intent "You've been researching dishwashers. Want me to shortlist the three that best fit your kitchen and budget?" rather than waiting to be asked.
  • Autonomous repeat purchasing. For known buyers with established preferences, AI agents will complete reorders, upgrades, and renewals proactively the buyer confirms with a single action.

The endpoint is a commerce experience where the effort required to find and buy the right product is so close to zero that the concept of "shopping" as a manual activity becomes obsolete.
