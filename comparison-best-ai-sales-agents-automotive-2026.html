Swirl

What it does: Swirl is a Touchless Commerce OS that deploys an autonomous AI sales agent on dealer and OEM websites. The AI handles the full sales conversation from engagement to conversion, including navigating the website, comparing vehicles, configuring options, calculating payments, and booking appointments. It operates via text and voice in 50+ languages and is trained on 100M+ real customer signals from YouTube, Reddit, TikTok, Amazon Q&A, and automotive forums.

Pricing model: Enterprise custom pricing based on deployment scope. No per-lead or per-conversation fees.

Best for: OEMs and large dealer groups that want a fully autonomous AI sales agent capable of handling the entire buyer journey without human intervention. Particularly strong for multilingual markets and brands launching EVs or new model lines where buyer education is critical.

STRENGTHS

Browser autonomy is the standout capability. The AI doesn't just chat. It navigates, configures, compares, and converts within the buyer's browser. No other product in this comparison offers this.

Training on 100M+ real customer signals means the AI can handle questions that go far beyond what's in the FAQ document. When a buyer asks about real-world EV range in cold weather, the AI draws on actual owner experiences, not corporate specs.

Go-live in 2 weeks. No replatforming, no multi-month implementation. The AI sits on top of your existing website and CRM infrastructure.

LIMITATIONS

Enterprise pricing means this isn't designed for a single-point independent dealer on a tight budget. The product is built for OEMs and larger dealer groups.

Relatively newer brand compared to established players like Gubagoo or Roadster, which means less brand recognition in the legacy dealer tech ecosystem.

Requires some onboarding investment to define brand guidelines, conversation parameters, and integration specifics, though the 2-week timeline keeps this manageable.

Key result: BYD deployment with Al-Futtaim achieved 27% engagement rate, 13% conversion rate, and 2.5x month-over-month AI session growth.

Gubagoo (Reynolds & Reynolds)

What it does: Gubagoo is a conversational commerce platform offering live chat, chatbot, SMS, and video chat for auto dealers. Acquired by Reynolds & Reynolds in 2021, it's now part of the Reynolds ecosystem and often bundled with Reynolds DMS and website products. The platform connects website visitors with live agents and uses scripted chatbot flows for after-hours coverage and initial triage.

Pricing model: Per-dealer subscription, often bundled with Reynolds & Reynolds DMS packages. Standalone pricing available.

Best for: Dealers already using Reynolds & Reynolds DMS and websites who want a tightly integrated chat solution without evaluating external vendors.

STRENGTHS

Deep integration with Reynolds & Reynolds DMS means data flows smoothly between chat conversations and the dealer management system. For R&R shops, this reduces integration friction.

Established brand in the dealer chat space with a large installed base. Many dealers have used Gubagoo for years and have workflows built around it.

Managed chat option means Gubagoo's own agents can handle conversations when your team is unavailable, providing a human safety net.

LIMITATIONS

It's fundamentally a chat platform, not an autonomous AI agent. The "AI" component is scripted chatbot flows that triage and route, not a system that independently sells. Complex buyer questions still route to human agents.

Being owned by Reynolds & Reynolds creates vendor lock-in concerns for dealers considering a DMS switch. If you move away from R&R, the Gubagoo integration advantages disappear.

Limited multilingual capability and no meaningful voice interaction support. In diverse markets, this is a significant gap.

Roadster (CDK Global)

What it does: Roadster is a digital retail platform that adds online car-buying functionality to dealer websites. Buyers can browse inventory, get trade-in estimates, calculate payments, apply for financing, and structure deals online. Acquired by CDK Global in 2021, it's now integrated into CDK's dealer website and DMS ecosystem.

Pricing model: Per-dealer subscription with potential transaction-based components. Often bundled with CDK dealer website packages.

Best for: CDK Global dealer website customers who want to add robust online buying workflows without changing their website platform.

STRENGTHS

Comprehensive digital retail feature set. Roadster's online buying flow is mature and well-designed, covering payment calculation, trade-in, F&I product presentation, and deal structuring.

CDK integration means the digital retail experience connects directly to the CDK DMS, creating a relatively seamless data flow from online deal to in-store completion.

Strong track record with large dealer groups. Roadster was a pioneer in the digital retail space and has refined its product over many years.

LIMITATIONS

It's a self-serve tool, not a conversational AI. Buyers navigate the digital retail flow themselves through forms, dropdowns, and calculators. There's no AI guiding them, answering questions, or handling objections. Drop-off rates in self-serve flows remain high.

CDK acquisition creates the same vendor lock-in dynamic as Gubagoo with Reynolds. The value proposition is strongest within the CDK ecosystem.

No voice support, limited multilingual capability, and no behavioral nudge system. The product waits for the buyer to start the digital retail flow rather than proactively engaging them.

CarNow

What it does: CarNow is a digital retail and messaging platform for car dealers. It provides online buying tools (payment calculators, trade-in estimators, credit applications) alongside chat and messaging capabilities. The platform aims to combine digital retail functionality with customer communication in a single interface.

Pricing model: Per-dealer subscription. Custom pricing for larger groups.

Best for: Mid-size dealer groups wanting a combined digital retail and messaging solution that's independent of CDK or Reynolds ecosystems.

STRENGTHS

DMS-agnostic positioning means CarNow works across different DMS platforms without vendor lock-in concerns. For groups running multiple DMS across locations, this matters.

Combines digital retail and messaging in one platform, reducing the number of vendors a dealer needs to manage.

Active product development and responsiveness to dealer feedback. As an independent company, CarNow can iterate faster than products buried within large DMS conglomerates.

LIMITATIONS

Smaller market share than Gubagoo or Roadster means a smaller support infrastructure and less leverage in product development compared to the DMS-backed competitors.

Digital retail features, while functional, aren't as mature or comprehensive as Roadster's. The payment and deal-structuring flows are solid but not class-leading.

AI capabilities are limited. Like most products in this space, the "AI" is scripted chatbot flows rather than autonomous conversational AI. Complex questions still require human intervention.

Drivee AI

What it does: Drivee AI is an AI-powered sales agent for car dealerships that handles conversations across chat, SMS, email, and social channels. It's one of the newer entrants in the space and positions itself closest to the autonomous AI agent category. Drivee uses AI to engage buyers, answer questions, and move them toward appointments and purchases.

Pricing model: Custom pricing. Drivee's pricing model is not publicly standardized.

Best for: Dealers looking for an AI-first conversational tool that works across multiple communication channels (not just website chat) and want to move beyond scripted chatbot flows.

STRENGTHS

AI-first approach means Drivee is genuinely building for autonomous conversation, not retrofitting AI onto a legacy chat platform. The product's direction is aligned with where the market is heading.

Multi-channel capability spanning chat, SMS, email, and social gives dealers broader coverage than website-only tools.

Newer architecture means fewer legacy constraints. Drivee can adopt modern AI techniques faster than platforms built on older infrastructure.

LIMITATIONS

As a newer entrant, the product is still maturing. Feature depth and edge-case handling may not match more established platforms.

No browser autonomy capability. Drivee can converse intelligently, but it can't take autonomous action within the buyer's browser (navigating, configuring, comparing). The conversation is still fundamentally chat-based.

Limited published case studies and performance data compared to more established players. It's harder to validate ROI claims without a track record of named-brand deployments.

Digital Air Strike

What it does: Digital Air Strike is a broader engagement platform that combines AI-powered chat, social media management, reputation management, and lead response for auto dealers. It's positioned as a comprehensive digital engagement suite rather than a pure sales agent or digital retail tool. The platform includes AI-driven chat (called "Response Path AI"), social media advertising, review management, and customer engagement tools.

Pricing model: Custom pricing based on modules selected. Dealers can choose which components of the platform to deploy.

Best for: Dealers who want a single vendor to handle both reputation management (reviews, social media) and conversational engagement, and value the bundled approach over best-in-class point solutions.

STRENGTHS

Broadest scope of any product on this list. Digital Air Strike covers conversational AI, social media, reputation management, and lead engagement under one roof. For dealers who want to consolidate vendors, this has appeal.

Strong reputation management capabilities. If managing Google reviews, social media presence, and online reputation is a priority alongside sales engagement, DAS bundles these together.

Long track record in the automotive space with strong OEM relationships and a large installed base.

LIMITATIONS

Jack of all trades, master of none risk. Because the platform covers so many functions, the depth of any single capability (including AI chat) may not match a purpose-built tool.

The AI chat component is not an autonomous sales agent. It's an AI-enhanced chat tool that triages and responds to common inquiries but lacks browser autonomy, voice support, and deep conversational selling capability.

The bundled approach means you may pay for modules you don't need. If you already have a strong reputation management tool and only need a conversational AI sales agent, DAS may not be the most efficient choice.

Podium

What it does: Podium is a messaging, reviews, and payments platform used heavily in the auto dealer space. It consolidates customer communication (text, web chat, social messaging) into a single inbox, provides tools for soliciting and managing online reviews, and offers payment processing. Podium is not automotive-specific but has strong auto dealer adoption.

Pricing model: Tiered subscription plans. Pricing is publicly available and ranges from standard to enterprise tiers.

Best for: Dealers whose primary pain point is managing customer communication across channels and improving their online review presence, with sales chat as a secondary benefit.

STRENGTHS

Best-in-class review management and solicitation. If your Google rating is a priority, Podium's tools for requesting, managing, and responding to reviews are excellent.

Unified inbox for all messaging channels reduces the chaos of managing text, web chat, Facebook messages, and other channels in separate tools.

Broad business applicability. Because Podium isn't auto-specific, it works across the entire dealership, including service, parts, and sales, with a single communication platform.

LIMITATIONS

Not an AI sales agent and doesn't position itself as one. Podium's AI capabilities are limited to basic chatbot triage and response suggestions. It doesn't conduct autonomous sales conversations.

Automotive features are generic because the platform serves many industries. Auto-specific capabilities (inventory browsing, configuration, deal structuring) aren't part of the core product.

No voice interaction, no browser autonomy, and limited multilingual support. Podium is a communication and reputation platform, not a sales conversion tool.

The result is a broad platform with over 8,000 clients across 51 countries, backed by $126M+ in funding.

Pricing model: Enterprise SaaS, custom by module. Pricing not publicly listed.

Best for: Large dealer groups that want a single vendor covering AI across sales conversations, lifecycle marketing, virtual vehicle walkarounds, and service retention.

STRENGTHS

Broadest feature set in the market. If you want one vendor handling AI-powered sales conversations, automated lifecycle marketing, virtual vehicle walkarounds, and service retention, Impel covers it all. No other single vendor offers this breadth.

TrueCar strategic partnership gives distribution access to 11,500 dealers. That scale means the AI is trained on a massive dataset of real automotive interactions.

With $126M+ in funding and approximately 450 employees, Impel has the resources and staying power for long-term enterprise relationships.

LIMITATIONS

Breadth comes at the cost of depth. The platform was built through three acquisitions (SpinCar, Pulsar AI, Outsell) that are still being integrated. The sales AI component is one feature among many rather than the core product.

Longer implementation timelines and higher total cost than focused point solutions. This is not a deploy-in-2-weeks product. Expect a multi-month rollout for the full platform.

No browser autonomy. The AI handles conversations but cannot take autonomous action within the buyer's browser to navigate, configure, compare, or convert.

Numa

What it does: Numa is an AI-native communication platform focused on answering every inbound call, text, and chat at a dealership 24/7. The company started in service department call handling (where missed calls mean missed repair orders) and has expanded into sales lead response. Backed by Google Gradient Ventures with $48M in total funding. Over 700 dealers including Fox Automotive Group, Longo Toyota, and Norm Reeves. Introduced a pay-for-performance pricing model where dealers pay only for appointments the AI books.

Pricing model: SaaS subscription with a pay-for-performance option. Dealers can pay based on appointments booked rather than a flat monthly fee.

Best for: Dealers whose primary pain point is missed phone calls and slow lead response, especially in the service department. If your BDC is overwhelmed by call volume and you're losing repair orders to voicemail, Numa solves that specific problem well.

STRENGTHS

Pay-for-performance pricing is a genuine differentiator. Most AI vendors charge a flat monthly fee regardless of results. Numa lets you pay based on appointments actually booked, which de-risks the purchase decision.

Strong service department focus. Over 2.2 million service appointments rescued and $1B+ in claimed repair order revenue saved across its dealer base.

Google Gradient Ventures backing and BMW/GM OEM certifications add credibility and open doors to certified vendor lists that dealers trust.

LIMITATIONS

Core strength is voice and call handling, not website sales conversion. If your primary goal is converting website visitors into test drive appointments, Numa addresses a different problem.

No browser autonomy. The AI handles conversations but doesn't navigate, configure, compare vehicles, or calculate payments within the buyer's browser.

Primarily English-focused. For multilingual dealer markets, the language support is limited compared to platforms built for global OEM deployments.

Toma

What it does: Toma builds voice AI agents for automotive dealerships. The AI handles inbound phone calls for service scheduling, parts inquiries, sales questions, and recall notifications 24/7. Backed by a16z (Andreessen Horowitz), Y Combinator, and Car Dealership Guy (Yossi Levi) with $17M in Series A funding. Founded in 2024, reached 100+ dealers through pure word-of-mouth before hiring its first salesperson. Named customers include Lithia Motors and AutoNation.

Pricing model: Subscription, scaling with AI agent operations. Specific pricing not public.

Best for: Tech-forward dealer groups that want to solve the phone problem first and are willing to bet on a fast-moving, VC-backed startup with strong product-market fit signals.

STRENGTHS

a16z backing signals serious investor conviction. Reaching 100+ dealers through word-of-mouth before any sales team existed indicates the product solves a genuinely painful problem.

The AI is trained on each dealership's actual call history, which means it gets smarter over time with dealer-specific knowledge rather than relying on generic models.

Already deployed at Lithia Motors and AutoNation, two of the largest dealer groups in the US. That's strong social proof for a company under a year old.

LIMITATIONS

Very early stage with under 50 employees. If you need enterprise-grade support, dedicated account management, and proven scalability at hundreds of rooftops, Toma is still growing into that.

Voice-only. No website chat, no outbound nurturing, no behavioral nudges, no browser autonomy. Toma solves one channel (phone calls) extremely well but doesn't touch the website.

Limited feature breadth. No vehicle configuration, payment calculation, or CRM intelligence capabilities beyond basic call data.

Fullpath (formerly AutoLeadStar)

What it does: Fullpath is an AI ecosystem built on a customer data platform (CDP) specifically for dealerships. It unifies data from CRM, DMS, website, and ad platforms into a single AI engine that automates personalized marketing, lead engagement, and sales outreach. Launched what it calls the industry's first "Agentic CRM" in January 2026 and pioneered MCP server infrastructure for automotive. Over 2,000 dealerships with $50M+ in funding.

Pricing model: Monthly SaaS per rooftop by module. Full platform estimated $2,000 to $5,000 per month.

Best for: Data-driven dealer groups that want their AI grounded in first-party customer data and are willing to invest in a CDP-first architecture that requires more onboarding but delivers more personalized engagement.

STRENGTHS

Only automotive AI platform built on a true customer data platform foundation. Every AI action is informed by unified first-party data from your CRM, DMS, website, and advertising, making conversations more relevant for returning customers.

Partnership with Gubagoo on the "Curator" unified intelligence engine shows Fullpath's data infrastructure is respected even by competitors.

At 2,000+ dealers with $50M in funding, Fullpath has the scale and resources to compete long-term and invest in product development.

LIMITATIONS

CDP-first means significant onboarding complexity. Expect a multi-week implementation to connect all data sources. Smaller single-rooftop dealers often find the platform over-engineered for their needs.

AI lead handling agent launched in 2025 and is newer than conversational AI from Impel, Conversica, or Numa. No browser autonomy, limited voice interaction.

Pricing in the $2,000 to $5,000 per month range puts Fullpath in the upper tier. ROI needs to be clear, especially if a dealer is already paying for CRM, DMS, and chat tools separately.

If you're interested in AI-first conversational engagement across multiple channels and are willing to work with a newer product, Drivee AI is building in the right direction.

Know which one you're buying.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best AI sales agent for car dealerships in 2026?

For OEMs and large dealer groups wanting fully autonomous AI, Swirl is the most capable option — with browser autonomy, 50+ language support, and a proven result of 27% engagement rate and 13% conversion rate with BYD Al-Futtaim. For Reynolds & Reynolds DMS shops, Gubagoo is the tightest fit. For CDK Global websites, Roadster is the established digital retail option. For dealers wanting AI-first multi-channel chat, Drivee AI is the most promising newer entrant.

What is the difference between an AI sales agent and a chatbot for car dealerships?

A chatbot is a reactive, script-based tool that matches keywords to pre-written responses and captures leads. An AI sales agent is autonomous — it navigates the dealer website, compares vehicles, configures options, calculates payments, handles objections, and books appointments without human involvement. The key technical differentiator is browser autonomy: an AI sales agent can take action on the website, not just respond in a chat window.

How does Swirl compare to Gubagoo for car dealerships?

Swirl is a fully autonomous AI sales agent with browser autonomy, 50+ language support, voice interaction, and behavioral nudges trained on 100M+ real customer signals. Gubagoo is a conversational commerce platform built around live chat, scripted chatbot flows, SMS, and video chat — primarily valuable for its tight integration with the Reynolds & Reynolds DMS. Swirl handles the full buyer journey autonomously; Gubagoo triages and routes to human agents.

How does Swirl compare to Roadster for dealerships?

Roadster is a self-serve digital retail platform that lets buyers complete online buying steps (payment calculation, trade-in, F&I) on their own. Swirl is a conversational AI that guides buyers through the same journey via natural conversation, voice, and browser autonomy. Roadster requires the buyer to operate the tools themselves — Swirl does the work for them while answering questions in real time.

Which AI sales agent is best for multilingual automotive markets?

Swirl is the only product in this comparison that offers full sales capability in 50+ languages, including vehicle configuration, payment calculation, and appointment booking in every supported language. All other products in this comparison have limited multilingual support — primarily English, with basic capabilities in a handful of additional languages.

What should dealers look for when choosing an AI sales agent?

The five most important evaluation criteria are: (1) Browser autonomy — can the AI take action on your website, or does it just chat? (2) Training data — is it trained on real customer conversations or just your FAQ documents? (3) Multilingual support — does full sales capability work in every language? (4) Go-live speed — can it deploy without replatforming? (5) CRM integration depth — does it push full conversation intelligence or just name and phone number?

Do any AI sales agents for dealerships support voice interaction?

Among the products in this comparison, Swirl, Numa, Toma, and Mia Labs all offer AI voice interaction, but with different strengths. Swirl offers native voice with full sales capability in 50+ languages — configuration, payment calculation, and appointment booking all work via voice. Numa and Toma are voice-first platforms focused on inbound phone call handling, primarily in English. Mia Labs handles calls and texts across sales and service. Gubagoo offers video chat (human-to-human), which is different from AI voice. Roadster, CarNow, Digital Air Strike, Drivee AI, Impel AI, and Podium do not offer meaningful AI voice interaction.
